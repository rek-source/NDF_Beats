01

Why Care Plans

What we sell, why it matters, and the one rule that never bends.

The NDF Home Care Membership is a yearly tune-up plan for the home — one membership, whole-home peace of mind. It makes Next Day Fix the homeowner's go-to team: front-of-line priority scheduling, an annual whole-home health inspection with a photo report, and included handyman labor hours on the higher tiers. It is not insurance and not a home warranty — it's a smarter way to keep small problems small.

What the homeowner actually gets

  • Priority "next-day" scheduling — front of the line whenever they need us, on every NDF service and KHB-family project.
  • An annual Home-Health Inspection — a licensed tech walks the home once a year and delivers a digital photo report.
  • Included handyman labor hours on Preferred & Total Home — real work done, not a coupon.
  • Priority storm / emergency response on the higher tiers — when something breaks at the worst time, members jump the queue.

The rule that never bends — no discounts

NDF does not discount labor in California. You will never say "member rate," "% off," "preferred pricing," or "waived fee." We are licensed general contractors and we charge the standard rate because that is what quality and warranty cost.

A Care Plan is not cheaper service — it's better access and more included service. You sell priority + included services (the inspection, the included hours, the front-of-line response), never a price break. If a homeowner is shopping purely on price, they are not our buyer — and that's fine.

Why we go door-to-door

  • Homeowners trust a real person on the porch more than a postcard. The membership is about an ongoing relationship — start it face-to-face.
  • Your beat is pre-scored: the homes on your list already fit the Ideal-Client Profile (owner-occupied, the right home age, the right tenure). You're knocking the right doors, so your job is the conversation, not the qualifying.
  • Every door you log — even a "not home" — makes tomorrow's beat smarter. The data you feed the system is part of the product.
02

The 5-Step Door Approach

A repeatable rhythm for every porch. Move through it in order — don't skip the trust steps to rush the close.

1

Approach & First Impression

First 5 seconds

Park the cart back. Walk up relaxed, badge visible, iPad down at your side — not pointed at them. Knock, then step back off the threshold so you're not crowding the door. Smile before it opens.

"Hi there — I'm [Name] with Next Day Fix, the licensed contractor that's been helping neighbors here in [City]. Did I catch you at an okay moment?"
2

Pattern Interrupt & Permission

Next 10 seconds

They're braced for a pushy pitch — be the opposite. Name the neighborhood, lower the stakes, and explicitly ask for a moment. Honesty disarms.

"I promise I'm not here to sell you a roof today. We just started a home-care program for a few homes on this street, and I've got 30 seconds to explain it — fair?"
3

Value Pitch

~60 seconds

Lead with the outcome, not the price. The membership keeps small problems small and puts them at the front of the line. Tie it to their home: an older home means more upkeep; a busy household means they want one trusted team. (Full script in Section 3.)

"It's a yearly membership — we inspect the whole home once a year, send you a photo report, and you go to the front of the line any time you need us. Priority and included service, not a discount."
4

Trial Close

Read the porch

Test temperature with a low-pressure question before you ever name a plan. A trial close surfaces objections early so you can handle them (Section 4) instead of getting a flat "no" at the price.

"Most folks on your block start with the Preferred plan because of the included labor hour. Does keeping one trusted team on call sound like something that'd help you this year?"
5

Book & Sign

Close the loop

When they're in, move smoothly to the iPad. Pick the plan together, open the agreement, and book the first inspection visit on the spot. Walk them through signing, then state the California 3-day right to cancel out loud (Section 5) — it builds trust and it's required.

"Perfect — let's get your first inspection on the calendar. I'll pull up the agreement here; it takes two minutes, and California gives you three business days to change your mind, no questions asked."

The Pitch Script

Three plans, framed by value. Memorize the structure, not the words — sound like a neighbor, not a robot.

There are three tiers. Anchor on Preferred (the middle) — it's the one most homeowners land on, and the included labor hour makes the value obvious. Present all three, but recommend Preferred unless the home clearly fits another tier.

Essential
$15 / mo
  • Priority next-day scheduling
  • Annual Home-Health Inspection + digital photo report
  • Priority access on all NDF agreements & KHB projects
Most chosen
Preferred
$30 / mo
  • Everything in Essential
  • 1 hour of included handyman labor / yr
  • Priority storm / emergency response window
Total Home
$69 / mo
  • Everything in Preferred
  • 2 hours of included handyman labor / yr
  • Annual Home Safety Check included

The script, start to finish

  • Hook (the problem): "Most folks don't have one trusted person for the dozen little things a house needs every year — the gutter, the running toilet, the door that won't latch. So small stuff turns into big stuff."
  • Bridge (the idea): "Our Home Care Membership fixes that. For a flat yearly membership, Next Day Fix becomes your home's team — we inspect the whole house once a year, send you a photo report, and put you at the front of the line whenever you call."
  • Present (the three plans): "It comes in three levels, and most folks pay monthly. Essential, fifteen a month, is the inspection plus priority scheduling. Preferred, thirty a month, adds an included hour of handyman labor and priority storm response — that's what most of your neighbors pick. Total Home, sixty-nine a month, doubles the labor hours and adds a full home safety check. Pay for the year up front and you lock in the best rate."
  • Frame the value (never the discount): "This isn't a discount card — we don't discount our labor, we're a licensed GC. What you're buying is priority and included service: the front of the line, the yearly inspection, and the labor hours that come with the plan."
  • Recommend: "For a home like yours I'd start with Preferred — the included labor hour usually pays for itself the first time you use it."
  • Move to action: "Want me to get your first inspection on the calendar? Takes two minutes right here."

Annual pre-pay is the best value — and that's not a discount

Members can pre-pay annually or pay per visit. Annual pre-pay "locks in the best rate" on the membership itself — that's a billing choice on the plan, not a cut to our labor rate. Frame it as: "Pre-pay the year and you're set — same great service, one simple payment." Never imply pre-pay buys cheaper labor.

04

Objection Handling

Acknowledge, reframe, ask. Never argue, never discount your way out of a "no."

Every objection is a question in disguise. Use the same three-beat move: acknowledge the concern, reframe around value, then ask a question that moves forward. Tap each one to see the scripted response.

"It's too expensive / I can't justify the price."

They're weighing the number against a vague benefit. Make the value concrete — and hold the line on price.

Say this

"Totally fair to ask. Think of it less as a fee and more as a yearly tune-up — the inspection alone catches the stuff that becomes a $3,000 repair if you miss it. And on Preferred, the included labor hour usually covers itself the first visit."

"If budget's the question, Essential at fifteen a month still gets you the inspection and front-of-line scheduling."

Never say

"I can knock some off for you."

"Let me see if I can get you a member rate."

"I'll waive the fee just this once."

"I'll just do it myself / I'm handy."

Respect the skill — then sell the time, the priority, and the second set of eyes.

Say this

"Love that — honestly half our members are handy. This isn't about whether you can, it's about your time and your priority spot when something's over your head or it's the holidays and everyone's booked out three weeks."

"And the annual inspection is a licensed tech catching the things that hide — roof, slab, water lines. Good backup for a hands-on owner."

Never say

"You probably shouldn't be doing that yourself."

"Tell you what, I'll discount it since you'll do most of the work."

"I need to ask my spouse / talk to my partner."

Often real, sometimes a soft no. Validate it, lower the risk with the 3-day cancel, and make the next step easy.

Say this

"Smart — it's a household decision. Here's the good news: in California you've got three business days to cancel for a full refund, so you can get your first inspection on the calendar today and still talk it over tonight with zero risk."

"Or if you'd rather, what's the best time I can swing back when you're both home?"

Never say

"This price is only good right now."

"Just sign — they won't mind."

"I'm not interested."

A reflex, not a decision. One gentle reframe, then respect the answer and log it — don't push a third time.

Say this

"No problem at all — most folks say that before they hear it's just a yearly inspection and a front-of-line spot, no obligation between visits. Can I leave you the one-pager in case it's useful down the road?"

If still no: "Appreciate your time — have a good one." Then log Not interested and move to the next door.

Never say

"Just give me two more minutes." (repeatedly)

Anything after a clear second "no." Respect it.

If you see a "No Soliciting" sign

Do not knock. Log the door as Refused in the app and move on. The beat list already flags known no-soliciting homes; honor any sign you see in person regardless of what the list says. (More in Section 5.)

05

California Compliance

The legal floor for door-to-door sales in California. This is informational — the binding terms live in the agreement.

The 3-business-day right to cancel

California gives a buyer the right to cancel a home-solicitation contract before midnight of the third business day after signing, for a full refund. You must tell every homeowner this out loud before they sign, and the signed agreement includes a written Notice of Cancellation with their copy.

This is a feature, not a hurdle — say it proudly. It lowers the homeowner's risk to zero and closes more deals than it loses. Do not summarize or re-word the legal text yourself. The exact, binding language is in the agreement; point them to it: the Care Plan agreement.

Solicitation rules — know before you knock

  • Honor every "No Soliciting" sign. If you see one, don't knock. Log Refused and move on. The beat already filters known flags, but a sign on the door always wins.
  • Identify yourself immediately. Name, company (Next Day Fix), and that you're a licensed contractor — badge visible at all times.
  • Respect local hours and "Do Not Knock" registries. Many cities restrict door-to-door hours and require a permit; only canvass during approved hours on your assigned beat.
  • Never misrepresent. Don't imply you were "sent by the city," that there's a "neighborhood mandate," or that an offer expires today. No false urgency, ever.
  • One clear "no" ends it. If someone declines, thank them and leave. Repeated pressure is both bad selling and a compliance risk.
  • Protect their data. Anything you collect goes only into the NDF Beats app. No photos of the home or occupants, no personal notes beyond what the door sheet asks for.

At the moment of signing — your checklist

  • State the 3-day right to cancel out loud.
  • Confirm the homeowner is the property owner or authorized to sign.
  • Make sure they get their copy of the agreement (it contains the written cancellation notice).
  • Choose the correct plan and billing option in the app; never alter the price.
  • Book the first inspection visit before you leave the porch.

When in doubt, stop

If a homeowner seems confused, pressured, or is a minor, an elder who doesn't understand, or clearly not the decision-maker — do not sign them. Note it, walk away, and flag it to your manager. A clean book of business is worth more than any single sale.

Role-Play & Ride-Along

Practice the porch before the porch practices you. Run each scenario aloud with a partner, then complete the ride-along checklist in the field.

Role-play scenarios

Run these in pairs — one plays the rep, one the homeowner. Swap roles. The coaching note tells the homeowner-player how to push.

Scenario A · Skeptic

"Another door-to-door guy."

Homeowner: Opens the door already shaking their head. "We don't buy anything at the door."
Rep target: Pattern-interrupt (Step 2). Lower the stakes, ask for 30 seconds, name the neighborhood. Do not pitch a plan yet.
Coaching: Homeowner-player — stay closed until the rep gives you a reason to listen. Reward honesty and brevity; punish a canned pitch by closing the door.
Scenario B · Price objection

"Four hundred bucks? No way."

Homeowner: Interested, but balks at Total Home pricing.
Rep target: Acknowledge, reframe value (inspection prevents big repairs; included labor pays for itself), step down to Preferred or Essential — never discount.
Coaching: If the rep offers any price break, "member rate," or waived fee — fail the rep on the spot. The correct move is to change the plan, not the price.
Scenario C · Spouse stall

"I have to ask my husband."

Homeowner: Warm, but won't commit without their partner.
Rep target: Validate it, use the 3-day cancel to remove risk, offer to book now or schedule a callback when both are home.
Coaching: Reward the rep who leads with the 3-day right-to-cancel. Penalize false urgency ("today only").
Scenario D · The close

"Okay, this actually sounds good."

Homeowner: Ready to move on Preferred.
Rep target: Move to the iPad, pick Preferred together, open the agreement, book the first inspection, state the 3-day cancel out loud, complete signing.
Coaching: The sale isn't done until the first visit is booked and the cancellation right is stated aloud. Mark the rep down if either is skipped.

Your First Day: Field Ops & Safety

Everything the porch doesn't teach: what to bring, how to dress, how to stay safe, and what to do when things go sideways.

What to bring every day

  • Charged iPad or phone with the NDF Beats app — it's your beat map, your logbook, and your scoreboard.
  • Printed handouts (the one-pager) and business cards — leave one at every warm door.
  • Water + electrolytes. More than you think you need.
  • Hat and sunscreen. You're outside for hours.
  • Closed-toe shoes you can walk 5+ miles in.
  • A charged power bank — a dead iPad ends your day.
  • Your ID and your NDF badge, visible at all times.

Dress code

Clean NDF shirt, neat appearance. You are a licensed contractor's representative at someone's home — look like the person they'd trust inside it.

Hours & territory

  • Knock the beat you're assigned in the app — the sequence is the route. Don't freelance into other territory (log true walk-ins with ＋ Log a door; module 08 shows how).
  • Typical knocking window: late morning to early evening. Your manager confirms the exact hours and any city permit rules for your beat.
  • Central Valley heat is real. Hydrate before you're thirsty, take shade breaks between blocks, and know the signs of heat exhaustion (dizziness, headache, clammy skin, nausea). If you feel them — stop, shade, water, call your manager.

Safety at the door

  • Stand slightly back from the door and off to the side after you knock — never crowd the threshold.
  • Never enter a home. Everything you do happens on the porch.
  • If a dog is loose or a situation feels off, disengage — skip the door, log it with a note, and move on. No door is worth an incident.
  • Two clear "no"s ends the conversation. Thank them and go.
  • Obey every "No Soliciting" sign (module 05 covers the compliance side).

Logistics

  • Parking: park legally at one end of the beat and walk it — don't leapfrog the car door-to-door.
  • Starting a beat: open the beat in the app and start at door #1; the walk order is optimized. Mark the beat's first door before you knock anything else.
  • Who to call: your manager is your first call for anything — safety, an angry resident, an app problem, a question mid-pitch you can't answer.
  • If the app looks offline: keep knocking. It queues every knock and sale locally and syncs when signal returns (module 08). Never stop working over a signal bar.

Pay & expectations

Your manager will walk you through the pay structure (base + commission) on day one. What's expected of you from the start: log every door you knock — including not-homes — work the full beat, and follow the compliance rules in module 05 without exception.

End of day

  • Check the app's pending counter — make sure it reads 0 pending (everything synced) before you go home.
  • Report your numbers to your manager: doors knocked, answered, sold.
  • Recharge everything for tomorrow.

Using the Beats App

A click-by-click of the tool in your hand — logging in, working a beat, recording sales, and staying productive offline.

Log in

  • Open the app, tap your name, and enter your 4-digit PIN (your manager sets it and can reset it any time).
  • Sessions last the workday; after a day rollover you'll re-enter your PIN. That's normal.

Pick your beat

  • The app lists the beats assigned to you. Pick the one your manager told you to work.
  • Your Walk-ins beat is always there — it's your personal bucket for off-beat doors you knock on your own initiative.

Read a door

  • Each pin on the map shows the door's Ideal-Client score and its last disposition; the list mirrors the map in walk order.
  • Doors flagged NO SOLICIT are marked — skip them (module 05).

Log a knock

  • Tap a door → pick a disposition: Not home / Refused / Callback / Not interested / Sold.
  • Add a note when it's useful — callback time, gate code, "big friendly dog." Your future self will thank you.
  • The sheet auto-advances to the next un-knocked door. Log every door, even the no-answers — the data makes tomorrow's beat smarter.

Log a walk-in / off-beat door NEW

  • Stopped in a nice neighborhood that isn't in a beat? Tap ＋ Log a door in the top bar.
  • Type the address, tap 📍 Use my location to drop the pin (optional — the address alone is enough), pick the disposition.
  • It saves just like a normal door and counts on your board. Doors log into whichever beat you have open — open your Walk-ins beat for true off-beat knocking.

Record a sale

  • Pick Sold → choose the plan — anchor on Preferred $30/mo (module 03).
  • The app opens the membership agreement for e-sign and first-visit booking.
  • Say the 3-business-day right to cancel out loud before they sign (module 05). Never discount labor (module 01).

Offline

  • Lose signal? The app keeps working: every knock and sale queues locally and syncs when you're back online.
  • Watch the pending badge in the top bar — before you leave for the day it must read 0 pending.

Your numbers

  • The top-bar scoreboard shows today's Knocked / Answered / Sold; the full team leaderboard lives on the Scoreboard page.
